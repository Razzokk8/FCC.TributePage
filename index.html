Bryant handling the basketball with the Los Angeles Lakers in 2014.

Kobe Bean Bryant was an American professional basketball player.
A shooting guard, he spent his entire 20-year career with the Los Angeles Lakers in the National Basketball Association NBA.
Regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time.

Here's a time line of some Kobe's achievement in Basketball:

  • 1978 - Born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • 1996 - Finishes his high school career as the all-time leading scorer in the history of southeastern Pennsylvania basketball.
    - Class-AAAA state championship.
    - National High School Player of the Year.
    - Naismith Player of the Year.
    - Selected by the Charlotte Hornets as the 13th pick in the first round of the NBA draft.
  • 1997 - Youngest player in NBA history at 18 years, 2 months and 11 days.
    - Named to the NBA All-Rookie second team.
    - All-Star Weekend slam dunk competition Winner.
  • 2000 - First NBA title with the Lakers.
  • 2001 - Second straight NBA title with the Lakers.
  • 2002 - All-Star Game MVP for the first time.
    - The Lakers win their third consecutive NBA title.
  • 2004 - Reached the NBA final, lost against Pistons.
  • 2006 - Bryant scores 81 points in the Lakers’ victory against Toronto.
    - Score league leader.
  • 2007 - Score league leader.
  • 2008 - Bryant wins regular-season MVP award.
    - The Lakers reach the Finals but lose to Boston.
    - Gold medal at the Beijing Olympics as a member of the U.S. men’s basketball team.
  • 2009 - Fourth NBA title.
    - NBA finals MVP.
  • 2010 - Fifth NBA title.
    - NBA Finals MVP.
  • 2012 - Second Olympic gold medal as a member of the U.S. men’s basketball team in London.
  • 2014 - Third on the NBA’s all-time scoring list.
  • 2016 - Scores 60 points in his final game.
  • 2017 - Lakers retire both of Bryant’s jersey numbers, No. 8 and No. 24.
  • 2020 - dies at the age of 41 due helicopter crash.
    - Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame.
